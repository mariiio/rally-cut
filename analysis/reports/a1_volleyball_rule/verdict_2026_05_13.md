## Ship gate

| # | Gate | Threshold | Result | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Panel same_team drop | ≥ 2 | -1 | **FAIL** |
| 1b | Panel correct rate | not worse | +1 (+1.0pp) | PASS |
| 2 | C-4 filtered drop | ≥ 80% | -95.0% | **PASS** |
| 3 | Fleet F1 not worse | ≥ baseline | panel correct +1, no new wrong | PASS |
| 4 | Abstention rate | ≤ 5% | 0.95% (panel) | **PASS** |

Gate 1 is the binding constraint. The plan requires same_team errors
drop by at least 2; A1 only flips 1 same-team error on the panel.

Looking at the 8 unflipped same-team errors in wawa: they are mostly
cross-team chain failures (the *team* assignment is wrong upstream, so
the same-team alt is ALSO wrong by GT), and the abstention bound at
0.3 norm distance leaves them on the current player. These are the
**non-cascade** wawa errors (chronic upstream team-confusion); A1 is
the wrong tool for them. The gigi cascade (1 of 1) does flip cleanly.

## Verdict

**NO-SHIP, infrastructure-ready.**

- A1 is wired and tested (9 unit tests pass) behind
  `USE_VOLLEYBALL_RULE_ATTRIBUTION` (default OFF).
- The hard volleyball-rule abstraction works: it cleans up 95% of the
  cascade-style C-4 violations fleet-wide with very low abstention.
- It does **not** clear the ship-gate threshold for panel same_team
  drop (-1 vs -2 required) — the remaining same-team errors are
  upstream team-confusion failures, not in-team mis-attributions.
- Keep the infrastructure default-OFF for now. Re-evaluate after
  - upstream team-assignment fixes (would unblock the 8 wawa errors), or
  - panel expansion (the binding constraint is the 105-action sample size
    — 1pp of correct rate is statistical noise on n=105).

`ACTION_PIPELINE_VERSION` stays at `v2`. No fleet refresh required.
