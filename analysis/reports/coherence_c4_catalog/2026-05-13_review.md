# C-4 Phase 1 → Phase 2 Gated Review

**Date:** 2026-05-13
**Catalog:** `2026-05-13.csv` (328 violations, 72 videos)
**Verdict:** **Phase 2 design DEFERRED until fleet re-tracked.** Phase 1 audit + catalog infrastructure SHIPPED and useful, but the absolute catalog counts cannot be trusted as a current-pipeline baseline.

## Finding (user spot-check, 2026-05-13)

The catalog measured the fleet's *current DB state*, which contains tracking output from a mix of pipeline versions. Many rallies were tracked with older code (before, e.g., the 2026-05-12 dual-signal serve-prepend guard, before the 2026-05-11 within-team proximity swap, before the 2026-05-10 classify_teams 2v2 fallback, etc.). The user spot-checked Card 1 (`match` rally) and several others, then re-tracked `mama` (one of the sampled videos) on current pipeline — **the C-4 violations disappeared on re-tracked output**.

The implication: **most of the 328 catalog entries are stale-tracking artifacts, not current-pipeline failures.**

## Known current-pipeline failures (Phase 2 test fixtures)

Two specific cases identified by the user during review survive on the current pipeline:

### F1: tata rally `724ead56-4093-48b6-8a78-fabc1a18fca1` — all-attributed-to-p1 cascade

Video `7d77980f` (tata), rally starts at 5:04.40. The rally has actions: `serve` (player 3), then `receive, set, dig, set, attack, dig` all attributed to player 1. The catalog flags **6 consecutive C-4 fires** on this rally — `receive→set`, `set→dig`, `dig→set`, `set→attack`, `attack→dig` — all same player.

Tracking is good and consistent. The attribution is the bug: every contact after the serve picks player 1 regardless of which physical player actually played the ball. Most likely root cause is upstream of `reattribute_players` — `_compute_expected_teams` cascading the wrong team chain, or `_find_nearest_player` repeatedly returning the same track ID for ball positions that are actually near different physical players.

This is the cleanest demonstration of the failure pattern Phase 2 is meant to catch.

### F2: kaka rally `918275cb-72b0-4f58-bcda-a89e351021c9` — serve mis-attributed to wrong player

Video `23f17802` (kaka), rally starts at 5:08.52. The `serve` at frame 260 is attributed to player 4; should be player 1 (per visual inspection). The `receive` at frame 320 is also attributed to player 4 (correctly — player 4 actually did receive), so C-4 fires because both consecutive actions are player 4.

The C-4 violation surfaces an *upstream* error: the serve-attribution is wrong, not the same-player-back-to-back logic. The right fix for F2 lives in serve attribution (likely in `_make_synthetic_serve` or the server-identity selection in `ball_features`), not in a C-4 repair pass.

## Phase 2 viability call

Given the stale-tracking-artifact caveat, the catalog's quantitative signals are not load-bearing for Phase 2 design. **Phase 2 design is parked** until one of:

1. **Fleet retrack + re-catalog.** Run current pipeline against the panel's 22 rallies (and ideally all 72 videos), regenerate the catalog, and re-assess. Expected outcome: ~10-50 C-4 violations on the re-tracked panel, not 328. THIS is the real baseline.

2. **Targeted Phase 2 design** using F1 + F2 as anchor fixtures, ignoring the catalog. Design a repair pass that handles the F1 cascade (multi-step same-player attribution cascade after serve) and validates against F1 + F2. A/B on the 22-rally panel as the only metric.

The audit infrastructure (Sub-2.B Phase 1 ships) continues to be useful as a *post-deploy* check — once the fleet is uniformly tracked, the audit's C-4 counts will be the real measurement.

## Recommendation

**Park Phase 2 design. Schedule a fleet-retrack workstream as the next prerequisite.** Phase 1's audit + catalog tooling stays in place; Sub-2.B as a whole becomes "infrastructure shipped, viability re-evaluated post-retrack."

Update the workstream status to `[PENDING — retrack first]` in MEMORY.md.
