## Decision: A/B candidate flag list for Plan Task 9

The two direction-signal gates (`direction_change_deg` 68.8%, `inflection_angle_deg` 66.5%) dominate. Together they contribute to nearly every case (large overlap expected — both measure trajectory bending at the contact frame). The two velocity gates (`velocity` 44.5%, `min_candidate_velocity` 30.6%) are a clear secondary cluster. `player_radius_depth_scaled` (23.7% overall) is dominated by serves: 17/34 serve cases (50%), 10/37 attacks (27%), low elsewhere — consistent with far-court servers being just outside the 0.15 radius.

A/B flag candidates in priority order:

1. **`RELAX_CONTACT_DIR_CHANGE`** — primary lever (top-1 gate, attacks/digs/receives/sets all dominated by it)
2. **`RELAX_CONTACT_INFLECTION`** — heavy overlap with #1; test second to measure marginal recovery beyond #1
3. **`RELAX_CONTACT_VELOCITY`** — pairs `min_peak_velocity` + `deceleration_min_speed_before` per spec; targets the 44.5%/30.6% velocity-gate failures
4. **`RELAX_CONTACT_PLAYER_RADIUS`** — for serves specifically (17/34 = 50% serve failure rate)

Skipped from the spec hierarchy:

- **`RELAX_CONTACT_WARMUP`** — early-rally-serve-specific. Not flagged as dominant in this probe; defer unless the per-action serve breakdown after the four flags above still leaves serves underperforming.
- **`SEQ_RECOVERY_TAU`** lower (classifier-stage) — the probe is generator-stage only; classifier-stage is not measurable from gate values alone. Defer; revisit only if generator-stage relaxations don't recover enough cases.
